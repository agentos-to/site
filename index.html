AgentOS — a local operating system for human–AI collaboration

Built for agents first. Inspired by J.C.R. Licklider (human–computer symbiosis) and Vannevar Bush (the Memex). Your data stays on your machine.

What it is

A local engine that runs a personal knowledge graph and speaks MCP, so any MCP-capable agent (Claude Code, Cursor, etc.) can use it as its tool surface. Skills connect the engine to services — GitHub, Google, iMessage, your browser. Apps render optional UIs on top of the graph.

The engine is a generic entity-and-relationship runtime. Everything service-specific lives in skills and shapes.

Who it's for

Right now: nobody. This is a research project, pre-release, with known security issues. If you're a researcher interested in human–agent collaboration, the architecture may be worth reading. Everyone else should wait.
